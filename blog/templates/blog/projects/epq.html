Extended Project Qualification

2019

Encryption: Should we put our privacy before our safety?

As part of my A-Level studies between 2017 and 2019, I completed the extended project qualification, which is equivalent to an AS level.

At the time, the 2017 Westminster Bridge terror attack was a big news story and so was the subject of end-to-end encyption. The attacker had used WhatsApp to send messages using this encryption methodology which investigators at the time believed were crucial to their investigation. Upon conducting further research, I found that this was not an isolated incident and there were many other similar cases around the world.

The government were unable to access these messages as WhatsApp prides itself on being totally private and secure- nobody, not even them, can access your messages.

I therefore felt that it was a very appropriate discussion to research, as there was a very fine line between privacy and security. These themes are explored within my dissertation (see below).
